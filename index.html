Energy-Saving Classrooms

Simple steps to reduce power use, save money, and help the planet.

Why Save Energy?

Classrooms use lights, fans/ACs, computers, and projectors. Small changes in habits and setup reduce bills, cut carbon emissions, and keep rooms comfortable.

Quick wins inside

Daily Habits

  • Use daylight: open curtains and sit near windows when possible.
  • Turn off lights, fans, AC, and projectors when leaving the room.
  • Set AC to 24–26 °C (or use fans first if weather allows).
  • Close doors/windows when AC is on to keep cool air inside.
  • Unplug chargers and devices not in use.

Room Setup

  • Use LED bulbs or tube lights (they use less power than CFL/halogen).
  • Seat plan: use front rows to reduce extra lighting needs.
  • Place projectors/screens away from direct sunlight to use lower brightness.
  • Label switches so anyone can quickly turn off the right ones.

Smart Device Use

  • Enable power-saving mode on PCs and laptops.
  • Dim projector brightness when lights are off.
  • Shut down computers after class; avoid long idle screens.
  • Use shared charging stations; avoid always-on extension boards.

Maintenance

  • Clean fans, AC filters, and projector vents regularly.
  • Report flickering lights and noisy fans; they waste energy.
  • Replace old appliances with 5-star rated ones when possible.

End-of-Class Checklist

Tip: Print this section and stick it near the door.

Illustrative Savings (per classroom)

Action What it does Typical saving
Switch to LED lighting Lower wattage for same brightness ~40–60% vs. CFL/halogen
Set AC to 24–26 °C Reduces compressor runtime ~3–5% per °C higher setting
Turn off idle projectors Cuts high standby draw ~50–150 W while off
Use daylight Fewer lights needed ~30–80 W per light avoided

Values are examples; actual savings depend on devices and usage.

Make energy saving a class habit—every switch counts.
